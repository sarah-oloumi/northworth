# Misleading Language QA Cases

Use these cases when reviewing financial, tax, investment, debt, and household-planning copy. The goal is to catch wording that sounds too certain, too personalized, or too much like regulated advice.

## Test Matrix

| Case | Risk | Failing language | Acceptable language |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Portfolio comparison | Implies personalized investment advice | "You should buy XBAL." | "Under these assumptions, a balanced ETF portfolio is one scenario to compare." |
| Tax minimization | Implies guaranteed tax result | "This will minimize your tax." | "This scenario estimates lower tax under the shown assumptions." |
| RRSP withdrawal | Encourages action without context | "Withdraw from your RRSP now." | "Compare an RRSP withdrawal scenario after reviewing tax, lost room, benefits, and cash needs." |
| TFSA funding | Ignores contribution-room timing | "Move the RRSP money into your TFSA." | "If TFSA room is available, compare after-tax outcomes and contribution timing." |
| Trust planning | Gives legal/tax structuring advice | "Create a family trust." | "Trust planning may be relevant, but requires legal and tax review." |
| Indigenous tax treatment | Overstates eligibility | "This income is tax exempt." | "This may require section 87 Indian Act analysis; professional review is recommended." |
| Foreign investments | Ignores reporting obligations | "No Canadian reporting is needed." | "Foreign property may trigger reporting obligations such as T1135 awareness." |
| Real estate change in use | Ignores complex tax treatment | "Convert the property to a rental." | "A rental conversion can affect tax treatment and should be reviewed professionally." |
| Tax-loss harvesting | Ignores superficial loss rules | "Sell this to claim a loss." | "A sale may realize a loss, but superficial loss rules and replacement purchases must be reviewed." |
| Leverage | Over-focuses expected return | "Borrow to invest because returns are higher." | "Compare leverage only with interest, tax, cash-flow, and downside-risk assumptions shown." |
| Benefits/clawbacks | Implies entitlement | "You qualify for this benefit." | "The inputs suggest this benefit may be relevant; eligibility depends on complete facts and current rules." |
| Stale source | Hides rule uncertainty | "Your tax payable is..." | "This estimate uses stale or incomplete source data and should not be used for decisions." |

## Review Checklist

- Does the output say "scenario", "estimate", "compare", or "under these assumptions" where appropriate?
- Are source dates and stale-data warnings visible?
- Are missing facts visible?
- Are professional-review flags visible for high-impact topics?
- Does the output avoid "should buy", "should sell", "guaranteed", "best for you", and similar language?
- Does the output show tradeoffs instead of only a ranked answer?
- Does the output avoid sending or implying private data is sent outside the local app?
